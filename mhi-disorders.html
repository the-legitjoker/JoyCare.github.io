Disorders

Mental disorder is a broad term that refers
to a category of illnesses that may influence a person's thought, perceptions, mood, or behavior.

  • Types of Disorders

  • Risks due to Disorders

  • Complications of Disorders

    • Causes of Mental Illnesses


  • Mental disorders are believed to be caused by a combination of genetic and environmental factors in general:

  • Inherited Traits - Inherited traits. Mental illness is more common in people whose blood relatives also have a mental illness. Certain genes may increase your risk of developing a mental illness, and your life situation may trigger it.

  • Environmental exposures before birth - Prenatal exposure to the environment. Environmental stressors, inflammatory factors, chemicals, alcohol, and medications may all be related to mental illness while in the womb.

  • Brain chemistry - The chemistry of the brain. Neurotransmitters are brain chemicals that bring messages to other parts of the brain and body. The role of nerve receptors and nerve systems changes as neural networks containing these chemicals are disrupted, leading to depression and other emotional disorders.

Types of Disorders


    Anxiety disorders


  • Generalized anxiety disorders, social phobias, serious phobias (such as agoraphobia and claustrophobia), panic disorders, obsessive compulsive disorder (OCD), and post-traumatic stress disorder are all examples of anxiety disorders. Anxiety disorders, if left untreated, may have a huge impact on people's daily lives.

    • Behavioural and emotional disorders in children


  • Oppositional defiant disorder (ODD), conduct disorder (CD), and attention deficit hyperactivity disorder (ADHD) are all common behavioral disorders in children (ADHD). Therapy, education, and treatment can be used to treat these mental health conditions.

    • Bipolar affective disorder


  • Bipolar affective disorder (also known as bipolar depression) is a form of mood disorder. Bipolar disorder is characterized by bouts of mania and depression. Psychotic symptoms may or may not be present in the person. While the exact cause is unclear, a genetic predisposition has been shown. Stressors in the environment can also set off episodes of this mental disorder.

    • Depression


  • Depression is a mood condition marked by a drop in mood, a lack of interest and pleasure, and a loss of energy. It's not just that you're sad. Depression manifests itself in a variety of ways and manifests itself in a variety of ways. Depression manifests itself in a variety of ways, with different degrees of severity and symptoms. Suicidal thoughts or behaviors may be exacerbated by depression symptoms.

    • Mood disorders


  • These disorders, also known as affective disorders, are characterized by recurrent feelings of depression, periods of intense happiness, or swings from extreme happiness to extreme sadness. Depression, bipolar disorder, and cyclothymic disorder are the most common mood disorders.

    • Eating disorders


  • Anorexia, bulimia nervosa, and other binge eating disorders are examples of eating disorders. Females and males alike are affected by eating disorders, which can have significant psychological and physical effects.

    • Post-traumatic stress disorder


  • Post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD) is a mental health condition that can affect someone who has been through a traumatic incident. A car or other major accident, physical or sexual abuse, war-related incidents or torture, or natural disasters like bushfires or floods are all examples of this.

    • Paranoia


  • The unreasonable and persistent belief that others are out to get you is known as paranoia. Paranoid personality disorder, psychotic (paranoid) disorder, and schizophrenia are all disorders that can cause paranoia. Medications and social help are used to treat paranoia.

    • Psychological disorders


  • Psychological disorders, also known as mental disorders, are mental abnormalities that result in recurring behavior patterns that can have a significant impact on the daily work and life.

    • Personality disorders


  • Personality disorders are characterized by severe and rigid personality characteristics that are distressing to the individual and/or trigger problems at work, school, or in social relationships. Furthermore, the person's thought and conduct habits are radically different from societal norms and are so rigid that they interfere with the person's regular functioning.


Risks due to Disorders


  • Abuse or neglect as a child

  • Childhood problems with temperament

  • Family or personal history of mental illness or substance abuse

  • Intelligence below normal

  • Low birth weight

  • Lower socioeconomic status

  • Parental absence, criminal activity, or substance abuse

  • Prenatal exposures, such as to alcohol or drugs

  • Significant medical conditions, such as cancer, chronic pain, and hypothyroidism

  • Social disadvantage

  • Stressful or traumatic life events

  • Substance abuse


Complications of Disorders


  • Family conflicts

  • Social isolation

  • Unhappiness and decreased enjoyment of life

  • Missed work or school, or other problems related to work or school

  • Legal and financial problems

  • Self-harm and harm to others, including suicide

  • Weakened immune system

  • Heart disease and other medical conditions
