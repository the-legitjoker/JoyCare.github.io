Disorders

Mental disorder is a broad term that refers
to a category of illnesses that may influence a person's thought, perceptions, mood, or behavior.

Signs and Symptoms of Disorders


    In terms of mental wellbeing, everybody has peaks and valleys. A traumatic event, such as the death of a loved one, can temporarily impair your psychological well-being. In general, the symptoms must cause substantial discomfort or interfere with your social, occupational, or educational functioning, and they must last for a given period of time to fulfill the requirements for mental illness.


    Excessive fear or uneasiness

  • Feeling afraid, anxious, nervous, or panicked

    • Mood changes

  • Deep depression, an inability to convey joy, apathy toward circumstances, feelings of hopelessness, excessive laughter for no apparent cause, or suicidal thoughts.

    • Problems thinking

  • Inability to focus or difficult-to-explain memory, thought, or speech disorders.

    • Increased sensitivity

  • Sensitivity to sights, sounds, smells, and touch increased; avoidance of overstimulating situations.

    • Withdrawal

  • Sitting for long periods of time and doing nothing, or abandoning previously enjoyed hobbies.

    • Feeling disconnected

  • Unreality is a vague sensation of being detached from oneself or one's surroundings.

    • Nervousness

  • Others' fear or suspicion, or a deep anxious feeling.

    • Unusual behavior

  • Strange, unusual, and unusual conduct.


    • Common Signs and Symptoms of Disorders


    • Suicidal thinking.

    • Sex drive changes.

    • Excessive anger, hostility or violence.

    • Problems with alcohol or drug use.

    • Inability to cope with daily problems or stres.s

    • Trouble understanding and relating to situations and to people

    Complications of Disorders


    • Family conflicts

    • Social isolation

    • Unhappiness and decreased enjoyment of life

    • Missed work or school, or other problems related to work or school

    • Legal and financial problems

    • Self-harm and harm to others, including suicide

    • Weakened immune system

    • Heart disease and other medical conditions
