    Treatment for Depression


    Depression is one of the most easily treated mental illnesses. About 80% and 90% of people with depression react well to therapy in the long run. Almost all patients experience some improvement in their symptoms.


    It is essential to treat depression as quickly as possible due to suicide thoughts as well as get relief from the symptoms. The following are some of the ways depression can be treated:


    • Medication – with the improvements in medication, antidepressants can assist in modifying the brain chemistry and produce improvements in some time.

    • Psychotherapy – the talk therapy can be effective in treating depression. Cognitive Behavioural Therapy is focused on problem solving and helps a person recognize negative thinking with the goal of changing these thoughts to more positive. It can assist in couple therapy or individuals alone.

    • Electroconvulsive Therapy – involve electrical stimulation to brain of individual to modify brain chemistry.

    • Avoid alcohol and drugs – For a short time, drinking or abusing drugs may make you feel better. However, these substances have the potential to exacerbate depression and anxiety symptoms in the long term.

    • Exercise - Aim for 30 minutes of physical activity 3 to 5 days a week. Exercise can increase your body’s production of endorphins, which are hormones that improve your mood.

    • Understand when to say no - Anxiety and depression symptoms may be exacerbated by feeling overwhelmed. Setting limits in both your professional and personal life will improve your mood.


    Natural remedies for Deprssion


      Essential oils

    • Wild Ginger - Inhaling the strong scent of wild ginger can activate serotonin receptors in the brain. The release of stress-inducing hormones can be slowed as a result of this.

    • Bergamot - Bergamot: This citrusy essential oil has been shown to help patients who are about to undergo surgery feel less anxious. Individuals who suffer from anxiety as a result of depression can benefit from the same benefit, although there is no evidence to back this up.

      • Vitamins

    • Vitamin B12 and B-16 - B-12 and B-6 vitamins are important for brain health. When your vitamin B levels are poor, you're more likely to experience depression.

    • Vitamin D - Vitamin D is known as the "sunshine vitamin" because it is obtained by sun exposure. Vitamin D is important for brain, heart, and bone health. Low levels of this vitamin are more common in people who are depressed.


    Dealing With Adolescent Pressures


    When teenagers are depressed, there are ways they should cope in order to prevent severe depression. All of these suggestions aid in the development of an essential sense of acceptance and belonging in adolescents.


    • Make an effort to make new friends. Healthy peer relationships are vital for a teen's self-esteem and provide a social outlet.

    • Involve yourself in sports, work, school events, or hobbies. Teens who stay busy are more likely to concentrate on constructive activities rather than negative thoughts or habits.

    • Become a member of a youth-serving group. Adolescent-specific services may assist in the development of new interests.

    • Seek assistance from a responsible adult. When issues become too much for a teen to tackle alone, he or she should not be afraid to seek assistance.

    • Irrational, off-the-wall behavior.

    • Overwhelming sense of guilt, shame or rejection.

    • Changed eating or sleeping patterns.

    • Severe drop in school performance.
