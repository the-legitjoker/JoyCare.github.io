    Sign and Symptoms of Depression


    • Sadness or a down state of mind.

    • Loss of interest or enjoyment in previously enjoyed hobbies.

    • Appetite changes â€” weight loss or benefit that isn't linked to dieting.

    • Sleeping problems or sleeping too much.

    • Increased fatigue or a loss of energy.

    • Increase in unintentional physical activity.

    • Feeling useless or remorseful.

    • Thinking, focusing, or making decisions are difficult.

    • Suicide or death thoughts.

    Recognizing Adolescent Depression


    • Poor academic results

    • Despair and hopelessness.

    • Withdrawal from friends and activities.

    • Lack of enthusiasm, energy, motivation or inspiration.

    • Rage and anger.

    • Excessive sensitivity to criticism.

    • Feelings of not being able to live up to ideals.

    • Shame or low self-esteem

    • Indecisiveness, inability to concentrate, or forgetfulness.

    • Restlessness and agitation.

    • Changes in eating and sleeping patterns.

    • Substance abuse.

    • Problems with school authority, police and parents.

    • Suicidal thoughts or actions.

    Recognizing Suicidal Warning Signs


    Four out of five teens who attempt suicide have given clear warnings. Pay attention to these warning signs:


    • Suicide threats, direct and indirect.

    • Obsession with death,

    • Poems, essays and drawings that see death.

    • Giving away belongings.

    • Dramatic amendment in temperament or look.

    • Irrational, off-the-wall behavior.

    • Overwhelming sense of guilt, shame or rejection.

    • Changed eating or sleeping patterns.

    • Severe drop in school performance.
